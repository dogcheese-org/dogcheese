How GGS Works

The GGS uses LIDAR, GPS, and other sensors to map the environment in real-time. This allows for automated micro-adjustments for stability, programmable geofencing to avoid hazards, and a simple "Return Home" function, drastically reducing pilot workload and required physical strength.

Safety First

Operator safety is paramount. The G4 Archangel is equipped with redundant engine systems to handle potential failures, a whole-aircraft ballistic parachute for emergency descents, and continuous real-time performance monitoring that alerts the operator to any issues.

G4 Flight Academy

No operator will fly without completing our rigorous certification program at the G4 Flight Academy. We offer tiered training for emergency, industrial, and recreational pilots, along with comprehensive maintenance plans to ensure every unit remains airworthy.

Go-to-Market Strategy & Roadmap

Our 5-year plan focuses on achieving certification, securing foundational contracts, and scaling production for market expansion.

Year 1: R&D and Certification

Focus on finalizing the G4 Archangel prototype and beginning the rigorous FAA certification process. Build out the core engineering and operations team.

Year 2: Initial Contracts

Achieve FAA certification. Conduct live demonstrations for target clients and secure at least two foundational contracts with emergency services or industrial partners. Begin initial production run.

Year 3: Scaling Professional Market

Scale production to fulfill initial orders and expand sales efforts within the professional market. Refine training programs based on early customer feedback.

Year 4: Entering Recreational Market

Launch exclusive sales to the high-net-worth recreational market. Establish partnerships with luxury and adventure retailers. Reach break-even point.

Year 5: Profitability & Expansion

Achieve strong profitability through scaled production and a dual-market (professional and recreational) sales strategy. Begin R&D for next-generation products.
